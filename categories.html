THE DIFFERENT TYPES OF TURTLES IN SRILANKA

Approximately 250 different species of turtles exist today. Both freshwater and saltwater are home to them. 7 of the 250 different species of turtles—out of which there are 250—live in the huge ocean and are referred to as sea turtles.

Out of these seven sea turtle species, five breed and reside along Sri Lanka's coastline. As follows:

1. HAWKSBILL TURTLE:

Eretmochelys imbricata is the scientific name for this animal. A hawksbill turtle may grow to a length of 24 to 45 inches and can live for 30 to 50 years on average. They weigh anything from 100 to 150 pounds. They are called hawksbill turtles because of their pointed, sharp jaws that resemble bird beaks and their top shell that has a heart-shaped structure. Another distinctive feature of them is their set of claws. The male hawkbills' tails are thicker and longer than those of the females.

2. GREEN TURTLE:

Chelonia mydas is its scientific name, and it is a herbivore. They are among the biggest sea turtles in the world. They have a maximum length of 5 feet and a maximum weight of 700 pounds. They belong to a species that is rapidly approaching extinction, and their numbers have been dropping over time. This species is called a "green turtle" because of the color and tone of its skin, which is somewhat green.

View Larger Map

3. LOGGERHEAD:

Caretta caretta is the scientific name for loggerhead turtles, which are carnivores. They are categorized as being the biggest of all sea turtles with hard shells. Its name refers to the fact that, compared to other turtles, it seems to have a huge head. They may reach heights of 36 inches and weigh up to 253 pounds on average.

View Larger Map

4. OLIVE RIDLEY:

Lepidochelys olivacea is the scientific name of the Olive Ridley Sea Turtle. They are both omnivores, like green turtles. In the wild, they may reach a maximum length of 2.5 feet and have an average lifespan of up to 50 years. A hundred pounds or more is about average for them. Due to both its carapace and skin color, Olive Ridley, it was given its name. Among the sea turtles in Sri Lanka, they are the smallest.

View Larger Map

5. LEATHERBACK:

Carnivores include leatherbacks as well. Dermochelys coriacea is their formal name. They frequently live up to 45 years and can get as big as 7 feet long. Average leatherback weight might reach 2000 pounds. The leatherback is said to be the biggest turtle on Earth and has been around for more than 100 million years.
